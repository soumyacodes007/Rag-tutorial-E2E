Vector databases play a crucial role in modern AI applications, especially in systems like
Retrieval-Augmented Generation. These databases store embeddings, which are numerical representations
of text, images, or other data types, enabling efficient similarity search.

When text is processed through an embedding model, it gets converted into a high-dimensional vector.
These vectors capture semantic meaning, allowing systems to find similar content even if the wording differs.
For example, "How to learn AI?" and "Ways to study artificial intelligence" may have very similar embeddings.

Popular vector databases include FAISS, Pinecone, and Weaviate. They support operations like nearest neighbor
search, filtering, and indexing, making them highly efficient for large-scale applications.

In a typical workflow, documents are first split into chunks, then converted into embeddings, and stored in a
vector database. At query time, the user's input is also embedded, and the system retrieves the most similar
chunks to provide relevant context to the language model.

This architecture enables scalable, fast, and accurate retrieval, forming the backbone of many intelligent systems today.
